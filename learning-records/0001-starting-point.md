# Starting point: comfortable with the what, new to the how

The learner disclosed their starting level when the course was set up. They are comfortable with what a database, an index, and a transaction are, and have used them as an application developer, but they do not understand the internals deeply. Their words: they understand most of these concepts "but not at a very deep level and not well enough," and what they most want is something that "ties the picture together."

Why it matters for future sessions:
- Do not re-teach the relational basics, SQL syntax, or the high-level idea of indexes and transactions. The difficulty floor is set above that.
- The highest-value move is connective tissue. Every lesson should locate itself in the whole engine and link to neighbours. The landing page is a deliberate map for this reason.
- Spend the depth budget on mechanism (pages, slots, pins, latches, splits, log records, versions) and on first-principles derivation of why each design exists.

Evidence: stated directly during goal-setting, plus the two-part goal they chose (deep intuitive mastery, and an MCQ exam with a viva on four papers). See [[MISSION.md]].

Implications:
- Retrieval practice is built into every lesson because the graded exam is MCQ only.
- The four assigned papers (architecture, Volcano, Bigtable, ARIES) get dedicated viva preparation, since the oral defense is on them. See [[RESOURCES.md]].
